Description

Homemade lasagna isn't as hard to make as it seems. Don't be intimidated by all the layers of delicious Italian flavor, try this homemade lasagna recipe today!

Lasagna is basically an edible construction project: you start with a baking dish and layer cooked lasagna noodles with rich tomato-meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta, and a sinful amount of mozzarella and parmesan. You keep stacking—sauce, noodles, cheese, repeat—until you've built a carb-and-dairy skyscraper. The top gets sealed with extra cheese, then it all bakes in the oven until the edges are bubbling, the top is golden, and the smell could make a monk break his fast. Let it rest a few minutes unless you enjoy burning your tongue, then slice into it like you're claiming your rightful portion of heaven.

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Onions
  • Canned tomatoes
  • Fresh herbs
  • Sugar
  • Noodles
  • Cheese
  • Eggs

Steps

  1. Cook the meat: Cook the ground meat in a skillet until browned and crumbly. Add the onion and continue cooking until it's translucent. Stir in the canned tomato products, half of the parsley, garlic, basil, 1.5 teaspoons of salt, oregano, and sugar.
  2. Cook the noodles: Boil the lasagna noodles in lightly salted water until they're al dente.
  3. Make the cheese layer: Mix cottage cheese, Parmesan cheese, eggs, the remaining parsley, the remaining salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  4. Assemble the Lasagna Layer the ingredients according to the recipe (starting with sauce and ending with mozzarella) until the lasagna is assembled.
  5. Bake the Lasagna Cover with foil and bake in the preheated oven for about half an hour. Remove the foil and continue baking until the top is golden brown.
